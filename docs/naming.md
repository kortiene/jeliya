# Naming — decision record

**Status: DECIDED 2026-07-05 — the project is renamed from "Bantaba" to
"Jeliya".** *Jeliya* is the Manding word for the art of the jeli (griot) —
the hereditary keeper of the community's true record, so that nothing is
quietly rewritten. Chosen from a screened shortlist (42 candidates
generated from the West African gathering/community register, 12 vetted
against registries, 5 shortlisted): verified meaning (Bamadaba; Hale 1997),
jeliya.dev/.app/.io unregistered at decision time (.com parked, buyable),
no software trademark surfaced, one adjacent-root collision noted
(PagerDuty's "Jeli" incident tool). Remaining actions: register the
domains, and run a formal TMview + Madrid + OAPI clearance before a public
launch push. The name honors a revered profession; the branding must keep
honoring it — calm, truthful, no caricature.

The research below documents why "Bantaba" was retired. It is kept verbatim
as the record of that decision.

## The former name "Bantaba" — trademark research record

Researched 2026-07-05 (multi-source, adversarially verified against primary
registries). This is a research record, not legal advice.

## Verified facts

- **Bantaba AB (Stockholm) holds a live, registered EU trademark** for the
  word mark "Bantaba" — EUTM 018569800, filed 2021-09-30, registered January
  2022, expiry 2031-09-30, no oppositions. The specification explicitly
  covers Nice **Class 9** goods ("Platform software", "Networking software",
  "Communication, networking and social networking software") and **Class
  42** services ("Development of computer platforms"), plus Classes
  35/36/38/45. Verified live against EUIPO eSearch. This materially weakens
  a "different product category" defense for EU-facing use of the bare name.
- **Their product is genuinely different** — a diaspora investment/talent
  matchmaking platform (~150+ startups, ~20 African countries, EN/FR site,
  documented West African activity) — but it is a software platform under
  the identical name, active as of July 2026, and its declared pan-African
  footprint encompasses this project's francophone West Africa target.
- **Identical etymology**: both names derive from the same Mandinka word for
  the village gathering place, which strengthens conceptual-similarity
  arguments in a likelihood-of-confusion analysis.
- **US register**: no Bantaba AB registration surfaced (inference from
  absence, not a full clearance search). The only live US "Bantaba" mark is
  an unrelated, incontestable restaurant registration (Reg. 5497041, Class
  43) — no class conflict, and mild crowded-field evidence. US common-law
  exposure still exists: free OSS distribution counts as "use in commerce"
  (*Planetary Motion v. Techsplosion*, 11th Cir. 2001), though the
  natural-expansion doctrine cuts the other way.
- **Domains (point-in-time, 2026-07-05, authoritative RDAP/whois)**:
  bantaba.com is actively operated by Bantaba AB; **bantaba.dev,
  bantaba.app, bantaba.io, bantaba.net, and getbantaba.com were all
  unregistered** and plausibly available.
- **Precedent**: Firebird (database) vs. Mozilla Firebird (browser), 2003 —
  category separation did not prevent the conflict, the keep-with-qualifier
  strategy failed in practice, and Mozilla renamed to Firefox within ten
  months.

## Unresolved

- Whether Bantaba AB holds WIPO/Madrid, UK, Swedish national, or — most
  relevant to the target region — **OAPI** (francophone West Africa)
  registrations. Check before any launch push in the region.
- No systematic sweep of npm/PyPI/app stores for other "Bantaba" software.

## Options on the table

1. **Rename now** — the research's synthesized recommendation: exposure
   today is low (small senior user, different products, generic cultural
   word, this project pre-adoption) but it is structural and grows with
   exactly the success the project wants; switching cost is near zero today
   and only rises.
2. **Keep + mitigate** — register the open exact-match domains immediately,
   avoid EU-facing commercial positioning under the bare mark, add clear
   non-affiliation language, obtain an EU + OAPI clearance opinion before a
   public launch push. A qualifier alone ("Bantaba P2P") is weak protection
   per the Firebird precedent.
3. **Defer** — decide at the first public-launch milestone; until then the
   exposure is negligible.

The visual identity shipped in July 2026 is name-light by design: the
meeting-tree mark carries no lettering, so only the wordmark component and
the static lockups (`assets/banner.svg`, `assets/og.png`) would change in a
rename.
